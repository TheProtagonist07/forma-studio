The Making — Light as a Material

We don't design these objects. We design the shadows they cast.

01 — Shadow studies

Candle, paper, camera

Before CAD, each pattern is mocked in cut paper around a real flame and photographed at 1/8 s — slow enough to see how the light wants to move.

02 — Translucency tuning

Wall thickness = brightness

At 0.8 mm amber PLA glows like honey; at 2.4 mm it smoulders. Every shade in this room is a deliberate gradient between those two numbers.

03 — The burn test

Heat, verified

Every flame-adjacent design runs a 4-hour thermal soak with a probe at the closest wall. Nothing ships warmer than 41 °C. Cozy must also be safe.

Failed Experiment № 9 — Kept

The first Ambra glowed too white. We reprinted the same geometry in three ambers and lived with each for a week of evenings. The middle one made the room feel like a held breath. That's the one you can buy.
